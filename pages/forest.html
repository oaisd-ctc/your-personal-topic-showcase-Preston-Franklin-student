Forest

Overview

Forests are categorized as a group of trees that surround a large area. They can be categorized by factors such as the type of trees and the density of the trees found in the forest. Some of the geograhpical features in forests include creeks and ravines.

Plants

Forests are mainly dominated by trees with diferent forests containing different kinds of trees. Most forests contain common trees such as Maple, Oak, and White Pine while others contain rarer trees such as Redwood. While uncommon, smaller plants and shrubs can be found on the forest floor as well occasional patches of moss. It is also common to find fallen trees lying in the middle of the forest floor.

Animals

The common animal found in forest is the squirrel. There are three kinds of squirrels found in forests which are the brown squirrel, black squirrel, and grey squirrel. Rarer animals include deer and rabbits. In extremely race cases, grizzly bears and grey wolfs can be found in large forests

Landmarks in forests

  • Ravine
  • Creek

    • A strange biome

    Very rarely a bog may form in the middle of a forest. Bogs are temporary swamp-like biomes that can occur after a heavy rainfall in a forest. They tend to be very muddy and small lakes that are very deep can be found in or around bogs. The wildlife doesn't change except for the addition of animals such as turtles. Bogs are smaller in size and they last for 3-4 months.
